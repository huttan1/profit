Articles and article groups

The article registry contains the base information of Litium Studio Product Catalog. From this single point, all information is updated and used in several assortments.

 

 

  1. All articles - Is a list of all existing articles.
  2. Article groups - Is a way to structure the article registry in a simple way. It might be the same as your ERP system, so the integration will be easy. One article can only exist inside one article group.
  3. Article - Are the foundation of Litium Studio Product Catalog. Contains a article number and several fields (Dependent on its field template). One article can be published in several product groups or assortments.
  4. Package - Is basically an article that bundle several other articles into a package. Example a “Sofa” has four “legs” and three “pillows”. They are all separate articles but together they will have a special price and a specific article number.
  5. Variant - A variant is an article that is attached to a variant group. A variant will share common information with the other variants inside the variant group. Example two t-shirts inside a variant group has the same describing text and image, but each variant has separate size values.
  6. Variant group - A group that contains common information for all variants that are attached to this group. A variant group can be published in several product groups or assortments.

An article does always exists in one, and only one article group. It will always exists one top article group, in this example “Articles”.

Manage articles, packages, variants and variant groups

 

 

All lists within the article registry are designed in the same way.

  • Article number - The article number are unique for all articles. A variant group does not have a article number, it consists of variant articles with their own article numbers.
  • Name - Name of article in current language (The language that you are using).
  • Type - If the row is a variant group, variant (An article that exists within a variant group), package (An article that consists of several other articles) or a article.
  • Remark-
  • Products - The first digit is in how many places this item are used (In assortment) and the second digit is how many of them that are published.
  • Status - An item can be complete or incomplete. An item needs to have all required fields (in all languages) filled in to be completed. Even though it is not completed in all languages, it can be published in all languages that are completed.
  • Template - What field template that are used.
Move items

A unique item (Article, variant, variant group or package) can only exists in one article group. Any actions that includes to move an item should be performed from an article group, not from “All articles”.

 

It is possible to move any item from one article group into another by using several actions:

  • Drag-and-drop the item from one article group into another. Select the item that should be moved and drag it into the correct article group, in the left menu. The article group should be selected.
  • Cut and paste several items from one article group into another. Select the items that should be moved and click “Cut” in the toolbar. Now select the new article group and click “Paste”. The items will be moved.
  • Copy an item. Any article, variant or package can be copied, one at the time. The item will be created with the possiblity to add a new unique article number.

Create products

A item (Article, variant group, variant or package) can exists in several product groups and in several assortments.

  1. To assign an article, variant group or variant to an assortment, select that item in the article grid.
  2. Click “Create products” in the toolbar and select from the popup-windows into what product group it should be created.

 

It exists several rules about how items can be created in assortments.

  • A item can can not be created several times in the same product group. This is because it would then have the same URL. Of course, a item can exists in several product groups in several assortments.
  • A variant group can not be created in the same product group as where the variant exists.

 

Add article

To add an article, follow these steps:

  1. Select what article group to what the new article should be stored. If it is created when viewing “All articles”, it will be stored under the top article group.
  2. Select “New” in toolbar and select “Article” or right click on current article group.

 

“Create new article” guide is displayed in two steps.

 

Add article - Step 1

Enter details:

  • Template - Select what field template to use for this article.
  • Language - In what languages should this article exist in. Fields that has multi-language support will only be visible in the languages that are choosen here.

 

Add article - Step 2

The second step will display all fields that are defined by current field template.

 

The first section of information contains general information for the article. The fields displayed are:

  • Article number - A standard field that will always be used and it is required. Can be maximum 255 characters.
  • Unit - A standard field that is used to determine how this article are delivered. Common examples are “pieces, kilogram, pounds”. If units should not be used, please visit settings for Litium studio Product Catalog to turn it off.
  • Display name - The field that are marked as “Display name” in current field template will be shown here. In this particular case, it is called “Name” and will be the same for all languages. However, this is a field setting that is dependent on your field template.
  • URL name - Add the URL name for a specific language. The full URL depends on where in a assortment the article is published.

 

 

 

 

Other sections of information depends on the configuration of current field template. All fields that are grouped (See settings for current field template) together will be displayed in a information area (See grouped information area in picture below) and fields that are not grouped together will all be displayed in a separate area (See common information area in picture below).

 

 

 

 

All required fields needs to be filled in before saving the article and they marked with a blue icon.

 

All fields that are required to have a value before publishing the article in an assortmen is marked with a green icon.

 

Click “Save” to save the new article.

Click “Cancel” to abort changes made.

Other sections of information depends on the configuration of current field template. All fields that are grouped (See settings for current field template) together will be displayed in a information area (See grouped information area in picture below) and fields that are not grouped together will all be displayed in a separate area (See common information area in picture below).

 

 

 

All required fields needs to be filled in before saving the article and they marked with a blue icon.

 

All fields that are required to have a value before publishing the article in an assortmen is marked with a green icon.

 

Package

To add a other articles into a package. See “Create a package” for more information. A package might be a couch package with one couch and four legs. The couch has its separate article number and the legs has its own article number.

 

Price and stock

Information about price and stock balance. Price information are only displayed if any price lists exists. Stock balance information are only visible if it is connected to a ware house.

 

Settings

Determine in which languages this article should occur in or to what field template it should be associated with.

Edit variant group

Find the variant group that should be edited by using following paths:

  • Search through using the “Quick search panel”. Perform a search by using the variant group name and select it. Click “Edit” found in the toolbar.
  • Filter the variant groups by selecting “All articles” in the left menu and unselect everything except “Variant group” in the filter area. Select the correct variant group and select “Edit” found in the toolbar.
  • Select correct article group in the left menu to display all articles, variants, variant groups in that article group. Select the correct variant group and select “Edit” found in the toolbar.

 

The “Edit variant group” page will be displayed along with three different tabs.

 

Edit variant group - General tab

The general tab will display all names in all languages that this variant group exists in. It will also display all variant group fields, in all languages that current variant group exists in. A variant group field are those who are common for all variant articles. Commonly used are description or image. See settings for current article template for more information.

 

Edit variant group - Variants tab

In this tab, all articles that should be connected to this variant group will be displayed. The information that will be displayed on each row are those who are article unique, for example size or color.

Two options exists:

  • Create a new article and attach it to variant group. (c). If this option is selected, a new row will be created with all fields in all languages. Common article information such as article number, display name, URL as well as article fields will be displayed in that row. When finished editing information, please select “Save” (a) or “Cancel” (b) of current row.
  • Add an existing article to current variant group. (d). Choose an already existing article that is not already connected to a variant group

 

 

 

Edit variant group - Settings tab

The settings tab will displau two options:

  • Template - Select what field template to use for this variant group. If the template are changed, all articles will be disconnected.
  • Languages - In what languages should this variant group exist in. Fields that has multi-language support will only be visible in the languages that are choosen here.

Create a package

A package is an article that has one or several articles connected to itself.

Find the article that should be edited by using following paths:

  • Search through using the “Quick search panel”. Perform a search and select it. Click “Edit” found in the toolbar.
  • Filter the article by selecting “All articles” in the left menu and unselect everything except “Article” in the filter area. Select the correct article and select “Edit” found in the toolbar.

When editing an article, several tabs are displayed. Click on the tab “Package”.

  2. The article will show up in the right panel, “Articles included in the article”.
  3. Click “Edit” to modify the quantity of that article.
